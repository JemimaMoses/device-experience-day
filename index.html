Purpose and Vision

Quality Services organization, with 1200+ employees across Chennai, Bangalore, and Arlington, provides a substantial pool of testers who can enhance end customer experience on new Amazon devices through timely and actionable feedback. This document establishes a framework for conducting Device Experience Day sessions facilitated by the QS organization, building upon the successful Play with Fire model. The framework outlines roles and responsibilities, risk mitigation strategies, measurement criteria, and scalability mechanisms to ensure consistent execution across all device launches.

Vision: Enable product teams to identify and resolve customer-impacting issues before launch through structured, efficient testing events that leverage QS organization's technical expertise and customer-centric perspective.

Program Overview

Device Experience Day is a time-bound testing event (typically 3 hours) where QS employees evaluate pre-release devices and provide customer-centric feedback to product teams. These sessions are conducted during Alpha/Pre-Alpha phases, ideally 2 weeks before Day 0, when product teams can still act on feedback to improve features and fix issues before street date (launch). Testing at this stage helps identify usability problems, confusing workflows, and customer pain points that might otherwise go unnoticed until after release. Sessions can be organized within 2 weeks and deliver insights within 3 days, enabling product teams to make decisions based on user feedback.

Testing Focus Areas

For Alexa Devices
  • Core Functionality: Alarms, reminders, timers
  • Media Experience: Music, video playback, streaming quality
  • Voice Interaction: Classic Alexa, enhanced AI models, wake word detection
  • Display & Navigation: UI responsiveness, menu navigation, visual quality
  • Camera Features: Image quality, video calls, motion detection
  • App Integration: Companion apps, cross-device features, account linking
For Ring Devices
  • Camera/Imaging System: Image quality, night vision, field of view, HDR performance
  • Audio System: Two-way audio, speaker clarity, microphone sensitivity, noise cancellation
  • Power Management: Battery life, charging performance, power consumption, low-power modes
  • Edge Computing/AI: On-device AI processing, object detection (people, packages, animals, vehicles), facial recognition, smart alerts, computer vision algorithms
  • Video Processing: Video encoding/compression, streaming quality, frame rate stability, latency measurements, pre-roll buffer
  • Cloud Integration: Video upload to cloud, event recording, live view functionality, snapshot capture
  • Mobile App Integration: Ring app connectivity, push notifications, real-time alerts, device setup/onboarding
  • Security & Privacy: End-to-end encryption (E2EE), baseline encryption, authentication mechanisms, secure boot, privacy zones
  • Smart Home Integration: Alexa integration (voice commands, announcements, routines), Ring Alarm integration, smart lighting coordination, chime integration, multi-device synchronization
  • Setup & Onboarding: QR code scanning, Wi-Fi provisioning, device pairing, firmware updates (OTA)
  • Performance Metrics: Response time, alert latency, app load time, video buffering

Virtual & Hybrid Considerations

  • Device Distribution: Ship to remote participants with return logistics and tracking
  • Facilitation: Video conferencing platforms with digital collaboration tools
  • Testing Window: 48-72 hour asynchronous window for time zone flexibility
  • Feedback Collection: Digital forms, virtual issue logging, scheduled check-ins
  • Accountability: Device tracking, signed agreements, scheduled return dates

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why should QS engage in Device Experience Day?

  • 1200+ employees with diverse skills and customer-centric thinking
  • Deep end-to-end product knowledge across multiple device generations
  • Constructive feedback grounded in real-world usage patterns
  • Early issue identification reduces post-launch support burden
  • Enhances QS team's product knowledge for subsequent testing phases

2. Why should Program Managers leverage this capability?

  • Access to large pool of technically sophisticated testers
  • Established processes handle recruitment, NDAs, logistics
  • Customer-centric perspective complements technical development view
  • Rapid feedback within 2 weeks (results in 3 days)
  • Structured analysis transforms raw feedback into actionable insights
  • Reduces post-launch issues and support costs

3. How is Device Experience Day different from Beta testing?

Aspect Device Experience Day Beta Testing
Duration 3 hours (time-bound) Weeks/months (continuous)
Timing Pre-Alpha/Alpha Beta through launch
Participants Rotating QS employees Stable participant pool
Focus Specific features/use cases Complete product experience
Goal Influence design decisions Validate quality over time
Turnaround 2 weeks to organize, 3 days for results Ongoing feedback

4. What about international/remote locations?

  • Time zone coordination handled through asynchronous processes
  • Device shipping requires 1 week lead time for customs
  • Geographic diversity provides valuable global market perspectives
  • No significant cultural/language barriers for QS teams

5. How much advance notice is needed?

  • Minimum: 10 days (T-10)
  • Ideal: 3-4 weeks for major launches
  • Breakdown:
    • 3-5 days: Participant recruitment and NDA collection
    • 2-3 days: Device preparation and configuration
    • 2-3 days: Location scheduling and materials preparation

6. What happens to feedback after the session?

Immediate (T+0 to T+2)
• Facilitator analyzes all feedback
• Deduplicates similar issues
• Identifies patterns and themes
• Categorizes by severity
Within 3 Days (T+3)
• Comprehensive report to PM and QS leadership
• Prioritized issue list with recommendations
• Positive feedback and improvement suggestions
• All issues tracked in JIRA/SIM
Ongoing
• Product teams access detailed issue information
• Facilitator available for questions
• Lessons learned inform process improvements

7. Can sessions be conducted virtually?

Yes, with considerations:

  • Device shipping logistics and tracking required
  • Virtual facilitation via video conferencing
  • 48-72 hour asynchronous testing windows
  • Digital feedback collection tools
  • Hybrid formats combine in-person and virtual participation
